Research

I recently completed my Master of Science degree at Brown University in the Behavioral and Social Health Sciences department. For my thesis work, I analyzed data from a sibling-comparison study of children whose mothers changed smoking behavior between pregnancies. I analyzed this data using hierarchical linear models with SAS, finding that deficits in inhibitory control are due to a spectrum of co-occurring environmental risk factors beyond smoking during pregnancy.

I have worked in the Center for Alcohol and Addiction Studies at Brown University School of Public Health since 2016. Currently, I coordinate data collection, recruit participants, and maintain participant databases for a randomized controlled trial for smoking cessation using positive psychotherapy. I am responsible for determining eligibility by using a structured clinical interview for DSM-V (SCID)

ClinicalTrials.gov

I am interested in research that examines the genetic and environmental risk factors which influence developmental outcomes in children. I am most interested in how early childhood experiences impact brain development and executive function across the lifespan.
